Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, many bidets are designed to be easily installed on existing toilets. You can choose from bidet attachments, bidet toilet seats, or standalone bidets depending on your preference and bathroom setup.

Typically, you will need a wrench or pliers to disconnect the water supply line, a screwdriver for securing components, and potentially Teflon tape to ensure a watertight seal on connections.

You need to turn off the water supply, using a T-valve to split the water line between the toilet and the bidet. Attach the bidet hose to the T-valve and ensure all connections are secure to prevent leaks.

Most bidet installations are straightforward and can be done DIY, especially for bidet attachments and seats. However, if you're not comfortable with plumbing work, hiring a professional can ensure proper installation and functionality.

Bidet attachments are generally compatible with most two-piece toilets. However, one-piece toilets with a sculpted or curved design may require specific models, so it's important to check the manufacturer's compatibility guidelines.
