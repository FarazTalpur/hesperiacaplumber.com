Frequently Asked Questions

There could be several reasons for a lack of hot water, such as a broken heating element, a malfunctioning thermostat, or issues with the power supply. Checking these components can help identify the problem.

If your water heater is leaking, turn off the power supply and water to the heater immediately. Check the pressure relief valve, drain valve, and the tank itself for any visible damage or corrosion that might be causing the leak.

It is generally recommended to flush your water heater once a year to remove sediment buildup, which can improve efficiency and extend the appliance's lifespan.

The U.S. Department of Energy suggests setting water heaters at 120 degrees Fahrenheit (49 degrees Celsius) to balance energy efficiency and safety, reducing the risk of scalding.

Noises such as popping or rumbling usually indicate sediment buildup at the bottom of the tank, causing the water to boil. Flushing the tank can help mitigate these noises.
