Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include strange noises, foul odors, leaks under the sink, frequent resets, and disposal not turning on.

To safely unclog, first turn off the disposal and unplug it. Use a flashlight to check for visible obstructions and remove them with pliers or tongs. Avoid using your fingers. Reset the disposal and test it again.

Loud noises often indicate that something is stuck in the disposal or parts are loose or broken. Turn off and unplug the unit before inspecting it for any foreign objects or loose parts.

While some people can install a garbage disposal themselves by following the manufacturer's instructions, hiring a professional is recommended to ensure it's installed correctly and safely, especially if electrical or plumbing modifications are needed.

It's good practice to clean your disposal every one to two weeks. Use a combination of ice cubes and rock salt or a designated disposal cleaner to scrub it. Additionally, citrus peels can help eliminate odors.
